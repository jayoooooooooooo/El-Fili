EL FILIBUSTERISMO
El Filibusterismo (The Subversive) is José Rizal’s darker sequel to Noli Me Tangere, published in 1891. The novel centers on Simoun, a wealthy jeweler who is actually Crisóstomo Ibarra in disguise, now bent on sparking a revolution after losing faith in peaceful reform. Through Simoun’s manipulations, plans for violent uprising, and the frustrations of students and common folk, the book portrays the escalation of resistance against oppressive colonial powers and culminates in a failed rebellion and tragic end that reflect the complexities of fighting injustice.
